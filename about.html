Necrofiction

In the time it takes
to flinch

Fast Twitch Fiction is a horror flash-fiction magazine for the short, wet, and unspeakable.

We publish necrofiction — body horror, cosmic grotesque, the divine and the diseased. Every piece is flash: it must be read in a single twitch, the involuntary contraction of a fast muscle before the brain has caught up. No build-up you can brace against. No mercy you can plan for.

The stories live in the magazine. This is the ward — dispatches, issue notes, calls for specimens, and the occasional field report from the slab. We treat each piece as evidence: filed, categorized, examined under coroner's light. We run three registers — the visceral prose that is our spine; the corrupted classified advertisement; and the conversational pitch that ends, flatly, somewhere you did not agree to go.

We are not interested in the spine-tingling or the chilling. We are interested in continence that won't take, in the host and the thing it hosts, in tissue that expands into galaxies. If a piece can be read aloud without the reader's jaw setting, it is not for us.
