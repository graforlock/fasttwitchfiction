Here is the thing we keep circling back to. Restraint doesn't work as a
weapon. It never has. You hold a thing back and back and back, and all you
get is a held thing — not a frightened reader, just a patient one.

So we stopped asking for it. We don't want the slow dread, the careful
build, the tasteful cut to black. We want the involuntary part: the fast
muscle that fires before the brain has signed off on it. The flinch.

> It doesn't work as a weapon, nor means to gather followers.
> <cite>on continence</cite>

What do you think? Is that a cheap trick — going straight for the body
instead of the mind? Maybe. We'd rather be cheap and fast than tasteful and
forgotten. Better than Power Slap, anyway.

Send us the piece that doesn't gel politely. We have a special glue for the
ones that do.
